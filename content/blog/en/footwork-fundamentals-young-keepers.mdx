Footwork is no longer optional. Over the past decade, the position has shifted: a modern goalkeeper must **build**, **slow the game down**, and sometimes **break the press**. None of that improvises itself at age 17. It is built from grassroots football onwards.

## Why start early

At age 8, the gesture is still plastic. This is the moment to install the right cues: support foot orientation, scanning before the touch, first touch with intent. A child who plays with their feet from football school becomes a comfortable 14-year-old keeper, while a goalkeeper discovering passing at 15 carries a gap that is hard to close.

<Callout type="info">
**Golden rule:** never sacrifice handling work for footwork. The two are trained in parallel, with equivalent volume up to age 12.
</Callout>

## Three key drills

1. **Passing in motion**: 5-metre relay, two touches, alternating support foot.
2. **Directional control**: coach feeds a ball, control toward a 1 m gate, then pass.
3. **Targeted long distribution**: find a specific player at 25 m; reward accuracy over distance.

## What to avoid

- Forcing long-range strikes before the mechanics are clean.
- Working footwork without gloves: they are complementary skills.
- Comparing your young keepers to pro models, since every profile builds its own style.

At the academy, we integrate these fundamentals from the U10 weekly session onwards. The goal: by age 14, footwork should be as natural as catching a high cross.
